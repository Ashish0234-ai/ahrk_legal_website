2. What types of civil matters do you handle?

We provide representation in matters such as property disputes, partition suits, contract enforcement, recovery cases, injunctions, succession matters, and other civil law disputes in accordance with statutory procedures.

3. What types of criminal matters do you handle?

We provide representation in criminal matters including bail applications, anticipatory bail, FIR-related matters, trial proceedings, appeals, revisions, and matters under special legislations such as the Prevention of Corruption Act, POCSO Act, and NDPS Act.

4. Do you handle writ petitions and constitutional matters?

Yes, we represent individuals, groups, and institutions in writ petitions and other constitutional remedies before the Supreme Court and High Courts, including matters relating to fundamental rights, administrative actions, and statutory compliance.

5. Do you provide advisory services in specialized areas of law?

We provide advisory and representation in areas such as cybercrime, banking and finance law, intellectual property rights, environment law, and alternative dispute resolution, in accordance with applicable legal frameworks.

6. What is Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)?

ADR includes mechanisms such as arbitration, mediation, and negotiation, which allow parties to resolve disputes outside traditional court proceedings. Our work in ADR is conducted in accordance with statutory provisions, including the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.

7. Can legal advice be provided online or over email?

We can provide general educational guidance online or via email. However, specific legal advice or representation requires formal engagement and cannot be offered without reviewing the facts and documentation of the matter.

8. Do you provide representation in environmental matters?

Yes, we provide representation and advisory services in environmental law matters, including compliance with statutory provisions, environmental clearances, pollution disputes, and matters before relevant authorities and courts.
